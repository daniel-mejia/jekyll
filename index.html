COACH

The Brief

The fitness industry is experiencing disruption due to advancement in technology. Today’s consumers are embracing wearable technologies and other activity-tracking products more than ever before. However, despite the availability of personal metrics, people continue to struggle to maintain a healthy lifestyle.

The Problem

We made interviews and a survey to gather enough data for our research. We wanted to know how people defined healthy living, their efforts to achieve that lifestyle, their goals, and frustrations. Our main takeaway was that most people want to live a healthy lifestyle but don’t feel motivated enough to make it a priority.

Root causes were:

  • Lack of motivation
  • Accountability
  • Accessibility

Survey findings:

  • 57% look to friends and family for fitness inspiration or information.
  • 67% wished that they had exercised more within the past week.
  • 55% identified a lack of time as the main obstacle keeping them from exercise.
  • 65% would train anywhere other than in the gym.

My proposed solution:

An app where you can book a coach to show you how to do any sport. It proactive recommends activities based on the user’s interests. Alert preferences are set in the on-boarding and delivered by geolocation.

Benefits

  • Encouraging users to enroll in exciting activities they can do on his own like diving, biking, and other gym alternatives.
  • Users can meet other health-oriented people and become more health aware by association.
  • The user will be alerted of activities that interests him nearby, wherever he goes.
  • People can learn at their own pace and convenience by booking any exercise available whenever they want.

Our research consisted of learning about the fitness technology industry, its main players, and also interviewing people. We created personas, journey maps, and wireframes. Solving the issue of motivation, accountability and accessibility was a team effort. Each of us designed low and mid fidelity prototypes to validate our concepts. We then tested high fidelity prototypes to test the usability of our concepts. Part of this exercise required me to design a style tile and a logo for the app. After doing research and testing our concepts, we gathered enough data to design a final product that tackles the issue of motivation, accountability and accessibility. I will soon share on my website, a case study with all the details about our final product.

Tools: Sketch, Photoshop, and Illustrator.
